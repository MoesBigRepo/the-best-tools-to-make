Workflows
05 · Build it. Run it.

Four ways to turn this on.

Two for builders (n8n, automated). Two for everyone else (no code, just clicks). Pick one for photos, one for videos. Each step is small. Each step is exact.

n8n · Builder mode

Photo pipeline, automated

Setup ~60 min · Each photo ~4 min

A robot does the work. You press one button. The robot writes the prompt, makes the photo, locks the face, polishes it to 4K, and emails you the link. You sit and wait.

Phase 1 · Get n8n running (15 min)
  1. Open a web browser. Go to n8n.io.
  2. Click the orange Get started for free button.
  3. Pick n8n Cloud. Type your email. Pick a password. Click Sign up.
  4. Open your email. Click the link n8n sent you. n8n opens.
  5. You see a big empty grid. This is your workspace. You are done with Phase 1.
Phase 2 · Save your three secret keys (15 min)
  1. Open a Notes app. Keep it open. You will paste keys here as you get them.
  2. In a new tab, go to console.anthropic.com. Sign in.
    1. Click API keys in the left menu.
    2. Click Create Key. Type the name "n8n". Click Create.
    3. Copy the key (starts with sk-ant-). Paste it in Notes. Label it "Claude key".
  3. New tab. Go to fal.ai/dashboard/keys. Sign in with Google.
    1. Click Add new key. Type "n8n". Click Create.
    2. Copy the key. Paste it in Notes. Label it "fal key".
  4. New tab. Go to aistudio.google.com/apikey. Sign in with Google.
    1. Click Create API key. Pick a project (or make one).
    2. Copy the key. Paste it in Notes. Label it "Google key".
  5. Go back to n8n. Click the gear icon at the bottom-left. Click Credentials.
  6. Click the orange Add credential button.
    1. Search "Anthropic". Click Anthropic API.
    2. Paste your Claude key. Click Save.
  7. Click Add credential again. Repeat for Google. Pick "Google Palm API" and paste the Google key.
  8. Click Add credential one more time. For fal.ai pick HTTP Header Auth.
    1. Header Name: Authorization
    2. Header Value: Key YOUR_FAL_KEY (paste the fal key after the word Key and a space).
    3. Click Save.
Phase 3 · Build the photo workflow (30 min)
  1. Top-left, click Workflows. Click the orange Add workflow button.
  2. At the top of the screen, name it Photo Maker.
  3. Click the big plus circle in the middle. Pick Trigger manually. (This is a button you press to test.)
  4. Click the small plus to the right of that node.
    1. Search "Anthropic". Pick Message a model.
    2. In the Credential dropdown, pick the Claude key you saved.
    3. Model: claude-opus-4-7.
    4. User message: Write a Flux 1 photo prompt for a professional headshot of a woman, 35mm lens, soft window light. Return only the prompt, nothing else.
    5. Click Test step. Wait. A written prompt appears below.
  5. Click the small plus on the right of the Anthropic node. Add an HTTP Request node.
    1. Method: POST.
    2. URL: https://fal.run/fal-ai/flux-lora.
    3. Authentication: pick the fal.ai HTTP Header Auth you saved.
    4. Send Body: turn it on. Body Content Type: JSON.
    5. Specify Body: Using JSON. Paste the JSON in Phase 4 below.
    6. Click Test step. Wait 30 seconds. You get a photo URL.
  6. Click the plus to the right. Add another HTTP Request for Magnific upscale.
    1. Method: POST.
    2. URL: https://api.magnific.ai/v1/upscale.
    3. Header: Authorization: Bearer YOUR_MAGNIFIC_KEY (get this key at magnific.ai/account after signing up).
    4. Body JSON: { "image_url": "{{ $('HTTP Request').item.json.images[0].url }}", "scale": 2 }
  7. Click the plus. Add a Send Email node (or Slack, your choice).
    1. To: your own email.
    2. Subject: Your photo is ready.
    3. Message: {{ $('HTTP Request1').item.json.url }}
  8. Top-right, click Save. Then flip the Active toggle on.
Phase 4 · The Flux JSON body (paste exactly)
  1. Copy this. Paste it into the JSON Body field of step 5 above. { "prompt": "{{ $('Message a model').item.json.message.content[0].text }}", "loras": [{ "path": "YOUR_TRAINED_LORA_URL", "scale": 1.0 }], "image_size": "portrait_4_3", "num_inference_steps": 35, "guidance_scale": 3.5 }
  2. Replace YOUR_TRAINED_LORA_URL with the URL from Workflow 2, Phase 3 (training your face).
  3. Save the workflow.
Phase 5 · Run it
  1. Open your Photo Maker workflow.
  2. Click the orange Execute workflow button at the bottom.
  3. Each node turns green when it finishes. Wait about 4 minutes total.
  4. Open your email. The final photo URL is there. Click it. You are done.

No code · Easy mode

Photo pipeline, by hand

Each photo ~10 min

You click. You wait. You save. No code, no setup, no robots. If you can use a web browser, you can do this.

Phase 1 · Plan the shot with Claude (5 min)
  1. Open a browser. Go to claude.ai. Sign in (free is fine).
  2. Click New chat at the top-left.
  3. Type this exactly: Write a Flux 1 prompt for a professional headshot. Subject: [type your model: hair color, eye color, age, vibe]. Style: 35mm lens, soft window light, neutral background. Return only the prompt. 60 words or less.
  4. Press Enter. Wait 5 seconds.
  5. Drag your mouse over Claude's answer to highlight it. Press Cmd+C (Mac) or Ctrl+C (Windows) to copy.
Phase 2 · Make the photo with fal.ai (5 min)
  1. Open a new tab. Go to fal.ai/models/fal-ai/flux/dev.
  2. Click Sign in with Google at the top-right.
  3. Click in the big text box at the top. Press Cmd+V (or Ctrl+V) to paste your prompt.
  4. Find the Image size dropdown. Pick portrait_4_3.
  5. Click the orange Run button. Wait 30 seconds.
  6. Right-click the new photo. Pick Save image as. Save it to your Desktop.
Phase 3 · One-time only: lock the face (25 min)
  1. Take 20 photos of the person whose face you want. Same person. Different angles. Different outfits. Good light.
  2. Drag all 20 photos into one folder.
    1. Mac: right-click the folder, pick Compress. You get a .zip file.
    2. Windows: right-click the folder, pick Send to → Compressed (zipped) folder.
  3. Open a new tab. Go to fal.ai/models/fal-ai/flux-lora-fast-training.
  4. Drag your zip file into the upload box.
  5. Type a "trigger word" in the Trigger Word field. Pick something weird so it does not collide with normal words. Example: ohwxmodel. Write this word down on paper.
  6. Click the orange Run button. Wait 20 minutes.
  7. When done, copy the URL it gives you (it ends in .safetensors). Save it in your Notes app. You will use it forever.
Phase 4 · Make face-locked photos (5 min per photo)
  1. Go back to fal.ai/models/fal-ai/flux/dev.
  2. Find the LoRAs section. Click + Add LoRA.
  3. Paste your .safetensors URL from Phase 3. Set Scale to 1.0.
  4. In the prompt box, start your prompt with your trigger word. Example: ohwxmodel woman walking on a beach at sunset, soft warm light, 35mm film, shallow depth of field
  5. Click the orange Run button. The face is now the same as your training photos.
  6. Right-click the photo. Save it.
Phase 5 · Polish to 4K with Magnific (3 min)
  1. Open a new tab. Go to magnific.ai.
  2. Click Sign up. Pick a plan ($39 / month).
  3. Click New upscale. Drag your photo into the box.
  4. Pick Upscale mode. Move the Creativity slider to 3 (low, this keeps the face safe).
  5. Click the orange Upscale button. Wait 60 seconds.
  6. Click the download arrow. You now have a 4K photo. You are done.
You need
A browser, $39 / mo
Skill level
5th grade and up

  2. Open hedra.com. Sign up. Click your avatar at the top-right → API. Click Create API key. Copy it. Save it in Notes.
  3. Open elevenlabs.io. Sign up. Click your profile at the bottom-left → API Keys tab. Click Create API key. Copy it. Save it in Notes.
  4. In n8n, go to Credentials. Click Add credential.
    1. Pick HTTP Header Auth. Name it "ElevenLabs". Header Name xi-api-key. Header Value paste your ElevenLabs key. Save.
    2. Add another. Name it "Hedra". Header Name X-API-Key. Paste the Hedra key. Save.
Phase 2 · Build the video workflow (40 min)
  1. Click Add workflow. Name it Video Maker.
  2. Add a Trigger manually node.
  3. Add an Anthropic Message a model node.
    1. Model claude-opus-4-7.
    2. User message: Write a 5 second Veo 3 motion prompt. Subject: the same model from my photo. Camera: slow zoom in. Mood: warm, cinematic. Return only the prompt.
  4. Add an HTTP Request node for Veo 3.
    1. Method POST.
    2. URL https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/models/veo-3.0-generate-preview:predictLongRunning?key=YOUR_GOOGLE_KEY
    3. Body JSON: { "instances": [{ "prompt": "{{ $('Message a model').item.json.message.content[0].text }}", "image": { "imageUrl": "PASTE_YOUR_PHOTO_URL_HERE" } }], "parameters": { "aspectRatio": "16:9", "durationSeconds": 8 } }
    4. Replace PASTE_YOUR_PHOTO_URL_HERE with a photo URL from your Photo Maker workflow.
  5. Add a Wait node. Set 4 minutes. (Veo takes time to render.)
  6. Add an HTTP Request node to fetch the finished video.
    1. Method GET.
    2. URL https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/{{ $('HTTP Request').item.json.name }}?key=YOUR_GOOGLE_KEY
    3. The response contains the video URL.
  7. Add an HTTP Request node for ElevenLabs voice.
    1. Method POST.
    2. URL https://api.elevenlabs.io/v1/text-to-speech/VOICE_ID (replace VOICE_ID with one from elevenlabs.io/voice-library).
    3. Authentication: ElevenLabs credential.
    4. Body JSON: { "text": "Your script here.", "model_id": "eleven_v3" }
    5. Response Format: File. Save as voice.mp3.
  8. Add an HTTP Request node for Hedra (lips moving).
    1. Method POST.
    2. URL https://mercury.dev.dream-ai.com/api/v1/characters.
    3. Authentication: Hedra credential.
    4. Body: send the Veo video URL and the ElevenLabs voice.mp3 together.
  9. Add a Send Email node. Send yourself the final talking-video URL.
  10. Click Save. Flip Active on.
Phase 3 · Run it (10 min)

  2. Click Execute workflow. Wait 8 to 10 minutes.
  3. Email arrives with a link. Click it. Download the file.
  4. The 4K upscale and color polish stay manual (no API exists). Use Workflow 4 Phases 5 and 6 to finish those two steps.

  5. In the prompt box, type exactly: She turns her head slowly toward the camera and smiles softly. Slow cinematic zoom in. Soft warm light. 35mm film look.
  6. Pick 8 seconds. Pick 16:9 (wide).
  7. Click Generate. Wait 4 minutes.
  8. When the video appears, click the three-dot menu → Download.
Phase 3 · Make a voice with ElevenLabs (5 min)
  1. Open elevenlabs.io. Sign up (free tier works to test).
  2. Click Speech in the left menu.
  3. Pick a voice from the gallery. (Or click Voice Library to browse hundreds.)
  4. Click in the script box. Type what you want her to say. Keep it under 30 seconds.
  5. From the model dropdown, pick Eleven v3.
  6. Click the orange Generate button. Wait 10 seconds.
  7. Click the download arrow next to the audio. Save it to your Desktop as voice.mp3.
Phase 4 · Make the photo talk with Hedra (8 min)
  1. Open hedra.com. Click Sign up. Pick the $10 / month plan.
  2. Click Create at the top, then Character video.
  3. Where it says Upload character, drag the video from Phase 2.
  4. Where it says Upload audio, drag voice.mp3 from Phase 3.
  5. Click the orange Generate button. Wait 5 minutes.
  6. Click Download. Save it to your Desktop.
Phase 5 · Polish to 4K with Topaz (10 min)
  1. Open topazlabs.com/topaz-video-ai. Click Buy now ($299, one-time payment).
  2. Download the installer. Open it. Follow the setup steps. (Mac users: drag the icon to Applications.)
  3. Open Topaz Video AI on your computer.
  4. Drag your video file from the Desktop into the window.
  5. On the right side, find Preset. Pick 4K Upscale (Proteus).
  6. Click the orange Process button. Wait about 5 minutes.
  7. The new file saves next to your old one with _4K in the name. Move it to the Desktop.
Phase 6 · Final color in DaVinci Resolve (15 min)
  1. Open blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve. Scroll down to Free Download. Click. Install. Open.
  2. Click New Project. Type a name. Click Create.
  3. Drag your 4K video into the Media Pool (bottom-left panel).
  4. Drag the video from the Media Pool down to the timeline (the strip at the bottom).
  5. Click the Color tab at the very bottom (it looks like a colored circle with sliders).
  6. On the right, find the node area. Right-click the empty space → Add Node → Add Serial.
  7. Drag the Lift wheel down a tiny bit (blacks get darker). Drag the Gain wheel up a tiny bit (whites get brighter). That is the film look.
  8. Top-left, click the Effects panel. Search "Film Grain". Drag Film Grain onto your clip on the timeline. Set Grain Size to small.
  9. Click the Deliver tab at the very bottom (a rocket icon).
  10. On the left, pick the YouTube 4K preset.
  11. Pick a save location (Desktop is fine).
  12. Click Add to Render Queue. Click Start Render. Wait.
  13. Done. The final 4K, color-graded, talking video is on your Desktop. Send it.
